# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One user — the owner. Uses this as a daily personal OS: logging workouts, tracking habits, managing tasks, and checking what's on for the day. Context is always a private browser tab, typically at a desk, at the start or end of the day. The job is fast, frictionless capture and a clear picture of the day — then get out of the way.

## Product Purpose

A single-user personal assistant dashboard that replaces scattered notes, habit apps, and calendar tabs. It surfaces today's priorities deterministically (no AI call on load), lets the user log anything in natural language via a persistent chat prompt, and shows progress over time (habits, lifts, tasks). Success means the user opens it, understands their day in under 10 seconds, and logs what they did in under 30 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Minimal. Precise.

Reference feel: Linear — dark, fast, no clutter, every element earns its place. Not sterile; considered. The UI should feel like the designer made intentional decisions at every level, not assembled from a template.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** (Notion, Jira, corporate blue) — too enterprise, too noisy, too much chrome
- **Fitness-app neon** (MyFitnessPal, Strava orange) — loud color, gamification badges, motivational filler
- **Productivity-app beige/cream** (Obsidian light, Bear, Things 3) — warm-neutral body bg is the 2026 AI default; explicitly avoided
- **Hacker terminal** (monospace, matrix green-on-black) — too costume-y, reads as "developer cosplay" not tool
- **Social-media loud** (red badges, notification anxiety, infinite scroll) — opposite of the calm this tool should project

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the hierarchy.** Numbers, dates, and names are the primary visual objects. Labels, borders, and decorative chrome support them — never compete.
2. **Every pixel earns its place.** No element without a job. If removing it doesn't break comprehension, remove it.
3. **Fast to read, fast to act.** The most common action on any screen should be reachable in one or two interactions. Layout encodes the priority.
4. **Calm confidence.** Motion and color signal state changes and progress — not excitement. Animations are short, eased out, purposeful.
5. **Private, personal, owned.** This is not a product someone demos. It should feel like a well-maintained personal tool, not a polished SaaS screenshot.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA — 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Single user, personal use. Reduced-motion media query respected for all transitions.
